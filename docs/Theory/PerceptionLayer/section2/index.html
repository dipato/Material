Goals

Introduction​

The use of sensors and actuators allows things to interact with the real world. In this session, the main concepts related to sensors and actuators will be explored by analyzing a series of examples that demonstrate their basic use. Before starting this session, it is recommended to review the following material to get contextualized, Interact with the phisycal world (lesson 3) from IoT for Beginners (link) of Microsoft.

Source: Components of IoT Apps, IoT-For-Beginners.

ESP32

An ESP32 development system (such as the NodeMCU-32S) is based on the ESP32 microcontroller, which features Wi-Fi and Bluetooth functionality. The development board operates at 3.3V and is powered through a 5V micro-USB connection or directly at 3.3V through the VIN pin. When using this development board, it is important to note that the GPIO pins are not 5V tolerant, and the maximum current they can supply is 12mA.
